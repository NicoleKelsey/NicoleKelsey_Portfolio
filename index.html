Case Study · Enterprise UX/UI

Internal Ticket Desk System

Client
Confidential Enterprise
Platform
Web (Internal)
Timeline
6 Months
Tools
Figma · FigJam

The Problem

Employees relied on a complex, form-heavy system to submit and manage support tickets. The existing experience lacked clarity, consistency, and meaningful feedback — resulting in widespread confusion around ticket status, ownership, and progress.

  • Unclear ticket statuses and ownership created accountability gaps
  • High cognitive load during ticket creation led to submission errors
  • Limited visibility into ticket progress and full thread history
  • No consistent design language across different views and roles

Research & Insights

  • Employees needed a guided, step-by-step form experience — not a single overwhelming screen
  • Agents prioritised fast scanning and status-at-a-glance over detailed read-through
  • Both groups needed clear feedback that actions had been registered by the system
  • Multiple user roles (submitter, agent, manager, admin) needed distinct yet consistent experiences

UX Process

01
Reviewing system requirements and constraints
Understanding technical limitations, existing data structures, and business logic before proposing any design changes.
02
Mapping key user flows for ticket creation and management
Documenting end-to-end journeys for both submitters and agents, identifying friction points and decision trees.
03
Designing wireframes to reduce cognitive load
Progressive disclosure, grouped form fields, and contextual guidance breaking ticket creation into manageable steps.
04
Iterating layouts based on usability and clarity
Multiple rounds of layout refinement focusing on hierarchy, component reuse, and clear status communication across all ticket states.

Luxury Automotive Dealer Platform

Client
Confidential Automotive
Platform
Responsive Web App
Timeline
6 Weeks
Tools
Figma · Prototyping

The Problem

Authorized dealers needed a secure and efficient way to purchase tools, spare parts, and licenses — but the existing experience was complex and time-consuming. The platform needed to feel worthy of a luxury brand while remaining fast and functional for professional repeat users.

  • Dealer users struggled to navigate a dense, poorly organised product catalog
  • Critical actions like licensing and checkout lacked clarity and confidence signals
  • The platform needed to balance high security requirements with genuine usability

Research & Insights

  • Dealers prioritise speed and efficiency — they are expert repeat users, not casual browsers
  • Most users follow repeat purchase patterns; browsing the full catalog is rare
  • Clear system feedback is essential at checkout and high-value payment steps
  • Visual language needed to signal trust, exclusivity, and premium quality throughout

UX Process

01
Analysed existing dealer workflows and business requirements
Mapped the full purchasing lifecycle — from authentication through to license activation and order tracking.
02
Defined user flows for browsing, purchasing, and order tracking
Streamlined key journeys to minimise clicks between intent and completion for expert dealer users.
03
Created wireframes to simplify complex product and checkout journeys
Progressive disclosure for product details, a consolidated cart, and a clear checkout summary with payment confidence signals.
04
Refined flows based on usability and stakeholder feedback
Iterated on cart, checkout, and order confirmation states to align both dealer needs and brand standards.

Case Study · Logistics UX/UI & Visual Design

Container Yard Management System

Client
Automotive Logistics
Platform
Web + Mobile/Tablet
Timeline
1 Month
Role
UX/UI · Visual Design

The Problem

The container yard relied on fragmented, manual, and disconnected processes — resulting in significant inefficiencies and severely limited visibility across operations. Staff on the ground had no reliable way to know the real-time state of the yard.

  • Manual tracking of container movements increased operational errors and dwell time costs
  • Limited real-time visibility of yard capacity and container status slowed every decision
  • Complex coordination between internal staff and external users (clients, shipping lines)

Research & Insights

  • Yard operators needed fast, visual status indicators — not data-heavy screens requiring interpretation
  • External users (clients, shipping lines) required simple, guided request workflows with zero ambiguity
  • Real-time status updates were critical to reducing operational delays and bottlenecks
  • Mobile-first task views were essential for yard and reacher operators working on the ground

UX Process

01
Mapped operational workflows
Documented the full container lifecycle — from vessel arrival through gate processing, yard storage, and departure scheduling.
02
Designed role-based user journeys
Separate flows for yard administrators, ground operators, and external clients — each with appropriate access and task focus.
03
Created low-fidelity wireframes
Rapid wireframing of dashboards, yard maps, container detail views, and mobile task screens before any visual design.
04
Validated flows with operational constraints
Testing designs against real yard workflows and edge cases — reefer containers, penalty tracking, emergency moves.
